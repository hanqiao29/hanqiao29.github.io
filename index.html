Hi there! I’m Han. I am a PhD student at University of Toronto Faculty of Information, advised by Christoph Becker. I am currently working in the intersection of human-computer interaction and urban planning.

I graduated from Wellesley College, studying math and media arts and sciences (cs + design). Both math and design are subjects that always keep me in awe and provide me unique ways to connect with other people. I then obtained my MSc in urban planning at University of Pennsylvania.

In 2021, I worked with Lydia Chilton and Vivian Liu at the Columbia University Computational Design Lab. This has been an incredible experience for me to delve a little bit deeper into human creativity and human-AI interaction.
My current research focuses on understanding how digital tools/data help (or not help) communities' sense making and decision making in urban planning process.
